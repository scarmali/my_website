Our Research

Pioneering innovations in protein-material interfaces for advanced therapeutic applications

Research Overview

Our research group focuses on the fundamental understanding and engineering of protein-material interfaces to develop next-generation therapeutic delivery systems. We combine computational modeling, synthetic biology, and advanced materials science to create predictable and responsive biomolecular systems.

Key Research Areas

Predictable Protein Modification

We use computational methods to predict and optimize protein conjugation sites,with both polymers and small molecules, enabling the rational design of protein therapeutics with enhanced stability, solubility, and pharmacokinetic properties. Our approach combines molecular dynamics simulations with experimental validation to create predictive models for bioconjugation outcomes.

  • Computational prediction of modification sites
  • Enhanced protein stability and solubility
  • Optimized pharmacokinetic properties
  • Rational therapeutic design

Responsive Protein Systems

Our team engineers smart protein systems that respond to specific biological stimuli such as pH, temperature, or enzymatic activity. These responsive systems enable targeted drug delivery and controlled release mechanisms that can adapt to the local cellular environment.

  • Stimulus-responsive protein engineering
  • Targeted drug delivery systems
  • Controlled release mechanisms
  • Environmental sensing capabilities

Nanostructured Therapeutic Carriers

We design and synthesize novel nanostructured carriers that can encapsulate and deliver therapeutic proteins to specific tissues and cells. These carriers are engineered to overcome biological barriers and improve the efficacy of protein-based therapies.

  • Novel nanocarrier design
  • Targeted protein delivery
  • Barrier penetration enhancement
  • Improved therapeutic efficacy
